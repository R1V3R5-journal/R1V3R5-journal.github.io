A journal for imaginary rivers

Studying riverine systems that do not exist

R1V3R5 is a journal for fake and imaginary rivers. The main focus lies in digital rivers: rivers in games, simulations, maps, image worlds, interfaces, and other computational media. However, we also welcomes work on rivers in stories and other written form (provided the subject is not simply scientific writing about actual rivers).

Read the journal

What counts as a river

The journal is interested in rivers as they are imagined, staged, drawn, simulated, remembered, or navigated through media. That includes rivers in fantasy novels, games, synthetic landscapes, mapping platforms, virtual tours, and other cultural or technical artifacts.

The scope is broad, but not unlimited. R1V3R5 is not primarily a venue for conventional scientific writing about real rivers unless that work is itself being treated as a textual, visual, or computational object.

Digital rivers come first

The editorial center of gravity is digital. Rivers in games, procedural worlds, simulation outputs, satellite interfaces, street-level image platforms, and similar environments are especially important because they show how river form gets translated into code, assets, rules, and viewing systems.

Those rivers may be unreal in a literal sense, but they still organize attention, movement, narrative, and spatial experience. They deserve to be read closely.

Essays and computational sketches both belong here

R1V3R5 publishes essays, close readings, annotated observations, and computational sketches. A small program, generated image, diagrammatic experiment, or reproducible toy model can be part of the argument rather than supplementary material.

The aim is not to separate interpretation from making. The journal should be able to hold both at once.

The site is part of the editorial method

Some of this work needs more than a static paper. Essays may require diagrams, moving images, layered notes, source references, or executable fragments to remain legible.

For that reason, the website is the primary medium. Web publication is not a cosmetic wrapper around a PDF; it is part of how the journal presents arguments, examples, and computational artifacts.

How the journal works

Editorial scope

Imagined, virtual, and mediated rivers

The journal looks at rivers in games, stories, interfaces, simulations, and related artifacts where river form is constructed rather than simply observed.

Main emphasis

Digital rivers first

Computational and screen-based rivers are the clearest focus, while literary and other cultural forms remain welcome when they support the broader editorial project.

Contribution types

Essays can include working artifacts

Textual argument, generated forms, and small computational experiments can appear together as one piece of scholarship.

Publication format

Web-native by necessity

The browser is the journal's primary medium because some arguments need layout, media, and executable elements that static export formats handle poorly.

R1V3R5 is still small by design. The intention is to keep the publishing system legible, let the format evolve in public, and publish pieces that treat imaginary rivers seriously enough to require their own editorial home.
